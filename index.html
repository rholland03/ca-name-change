This site will help you change your name after marriage in California, step-by-step, with county-specific resources.

Step-by-Step: How to Change Your Name in California

1

Obtain your marriage certificate

Request multiple certified copies from the county where you were married — you'll need them for Social Security, DMV, banks, and other records.

2

Decide your new name

Think about whether you want your spouse’s last name, a hyphenated name, or to keep your current name. Consistency across legal documents is key.

3

Update your Social Security record

Fill out the SS-5 form and submit it with your marriage certificate. This ensures taxes and benefits are correct.

4

Update your California driver’s license or ID

Schedule a DMV appointment online to save time. Bring your updated Social Security card and marriage certificate.

5

Update bank accounts, credit cards, and employer records

Make a checklist of all accounts (banks, credit cards, utilities, insurance, employer HR). Bring certified copies of your marriage certificate if required.

6

Check with your county clerk/recorder

Some counties require extra local paperwork. Use the county resources below to verify requirements.
